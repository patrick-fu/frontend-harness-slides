# Slide Copy Quality

Use this as a lightweight final copy pass. The goal is to remove generic,
mechanical writing while preserving facts, numbers, and the user's intent.

## Rules

- Keep slide titles specific enough to make sense on their own.
- Replace vague claims with concrete evidence, numbers, examples, or direct
  takeaways.
- Remove duplicated phrases and symmetrical filler.
- For speaker-led decks, keep on-slide copy short and let the spoken narrative
  carry detail.
- For reading-first decks, make each slide self-contained without shrinking text
  into unreadability.
- Do not add hype words to compensate for weak content; clarify the point
  instead.

## Examples

| Scenario | Weak | Better |
|---|---|---|
| Generic claim | Unlock seamless collaboration | Cut review loops from 3 days to 1 |
| Empty title | Our solution | What changes after the workflow is automated |
| Filler | Robust, scalable, and user-friendly | Handles 10k records without manual cleanup |
