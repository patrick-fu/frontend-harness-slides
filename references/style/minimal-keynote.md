# Minimal Keynote Styles (Styles 1 - 8)

Minimal Keynote styles are sparse, high-impact, and presentation-led. They are designed for live talks, openings, section transitions, and big memorable claims where the speaker wants the audience's attention focused on a single concept or sentence.

"Audience gets the point in three seconds."

---

## Style 01: Apple Launch Mono

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A premium, expensive, and highly focused product launch screen. It uses extreme whitespace, a single high-fidelity central object, and dramatic typographic scale to convey quiet confidence.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Pure off-white (`#f5f5f7`) or deep space black (`#1d1d1f`). No background patterns or grids.
- **Ink**: High-contrast black (`#1d1d1f`) or pure white (`#ffffff`). Accent colors are used sparingly (less than 5% of the screen).
- **Typography**: Large, elegant serif or high-end sans-serif display font (e.g., Playfair Display, Inter Extra Bold). The font size ratio between the title and supporting text should be at least 4:1 to create a dramatic typographic hierarchy.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Golden Sentence**: A single, powerful line of text positioned in the upper-middle of the screen, with a tiny, muted mono label at the very top.
- **The Spotlight Metaphor**: A single, high-fidelity central visual object (like a glowing microphone or a rotating device) centered on the screen, with a short, elegant title underneath.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Opening Thesis**, **Section Openers**, and **Big Claims**. Use this when you want to make a single, unforgettable statement.

## Style 02: Sketch Board Emoji

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
An interactive, warm, and approachable creative workshop whiteboard. It mimics a physical brainstorming session with hand-drawn lines, sticky notes, tape, and emoji actors, making complex workflows feel human-in-the-loop.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Warm, recycled paper white (`#fcfbf7`) with a very light, subtle grid pattern (`rgba(0,0,0,0.03)`).
- **Ink**: Natural charcoal black (`#2d2722`) for lines and text. Accent colors are warm and organic (e.g., sticky-note yellow `#fef08a`, tape blue `#bfdbfe`, risk red `#fecaca`).
- **Typography**: A warm, handwriting or sketch-like font (e.g., Caveat, local CJK hand-drawn fallbacks) for titles and annotations, paired with a clean sans-serif for body text.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Sticky Note Wall**: A collection of slightly rotated, overlapping sticky notes with hand-drawn borders (`sketch-border`), connected by loose arrows.
- **The Emoji Theatre**: Large emoji actors positioned around a central sketch box, with hand-drawn speech bubbles explaining their roles and interactions.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Collaboration Concepts**, **Messy-to-Structured Transitions**, and **Human-Agent Workflows**. Use this to make technical processes feel friendly and approachable.

## Style 03: Interactive Dialogue Stage

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A tiny, dramatic theater scene or chat console where the interaction between two roles (e.g., human and agent, or client and server) is staged. It focuses on the dialogue, questions, and responses.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Deep, dark stage blue (`#0f172a` or `#0b0f19`).
- **Ink**: Crisp white for text, with teal (`#0d9488`) or emerald (`#10b981`) for the active speaker, and slate (`#64748b`) for the background.
- **Typography**: Clean, modern monospaced fonts (e.g., Fira Code, local system mono) to emphasize the digital, structured nature of the dialogue.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Chat Thread**: A vertical, non-symmetric sequence of speech bubbles. The human speaker is aligned to the left with a warm avatar, while the agent/system is aligned to the right with a glowing digital avatar.
- **The Dual-Console Split**: A split-screen layout where the left side shows the input command/speech, and the right side shows the system's real-time reasoning and response.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Interactive Dialogues**, **人机协作 (Human-Agent Collaboration)**, and **Clarifying Questions**. Use this to show why asking questions before acting is safer.

## Style 04: Kinetic Type Punchline

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A bold, high-energy graphic poster. It uses stacked, massive, high-impact words to deliver a punchline or a core argument with maximum graphic energy.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Solid black (`#000000`) or high-saturation yellow (`#facc15`).
- **Ink**: Pure white, black, or neon yellow. Extreme contrast is mandatory.
- **Typography**: Massive, extra-bold, condensed sans-serif fonts (e.g., Inter Extra Bold, Impact). Text is often uppercase, with tight line height and letter spacing.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Stacked Poster**: 3 to 4 massive words stacked vertically, taking up 80% of the screen, with a single line of tiny, high-contrast explanatory text at the bottom.
- **The Typographic Strike**: A massive golden sentence with a key word crossed out (`line-through`) and replaced by a high-contrast, glowing word in a different color.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Section Transitions**, **Memorable Contrasts**, and **High-Energy Keynote Beats**. Use this to wake up the audience and deliver a definitive claim.

## Style 05: Object Metaphor Hero

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A tactile, physical kit or package (like a field kit, a toolbox, or a packed backpack). It represents the idea of preparation, structured planning, and carrying the right tools before embarking on a journey.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Warm, textured cream (`#fffbeb`) or light sand.
- **Ink**: Rich amber (`#78350f`), dark brown, and warm gold.
- **Typography**: Clean, structured sans-serif (e.g., Inter) for labels, paired with a classic serif for titles.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Compartment Grid**: A beautifully organized, tactile grid representing a "backpack" or "kit", where each compartment holds a specific visual asset (represented by an emoji or icon) and a short label.
- **The Hero Object**: A single, large, central illustration of a physical object (e.g., a compass or a lantern), with radiating lines pointing to different supporting concepts.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Structured Preparation**, **Toolkits**, and **Abstract Concept Simplification**. Use this to explain why preparing context is better than rushing into execution.

## Style 06: Blackboard Chalk Talk

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A classroom or engineering blackboard where a teacher or engineer is actively proving a formula or drawing a conceptual diagram. It feels handmade, educational, and reasoning-first.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Deep chalkboard green (`#1e2923`) or dark slate.
- **Ink**: Chalk white, pastel yellow (`#fef08a`), and soft mint green (`#a7f3d0`). Lines should look like chalk strokes.
- **Typography**: A thin, handwritten font (e.g., Caveat) paired with a clean, light monospaced font for technical formulas.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Formula Proof**: A large, hand-drawn mathematical or conceptual formula centered on the board, with chalk-drawn arrows pointing to explanatory margin notes.
- **The Conceptual Sketch**: A simple, hand-drawn block diagram showing how multiple variables interact to produce a safe result.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Core Principles**, **Conceptual Formulas**, and **Educational Explanations**. Use this to show the logical reasoning behind a design decision.

## Style 07: Arcade Boss Fight

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A retro 8-bit arcade game screen. It frames a technical challenge as a "boss fight" and the preparation of context as "collecting power-ups", making engineering risks feel playful and engaging.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Pure black (`#000000`).
- **Ink**: Neon green (`#22c55e`), vibrant red (`#ef4444`), and arcade yellow (`#eab308`).
- **Typography**: Pixelated arcade fonts (e.g., Press Start 2P, VT323).

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Battle Screen**: A classic game layout showing "Player HP" (representing the developer or system) and "Boss HP" (representing the legacy codebase or technical debt), with status bars and pixelated icons.
- **The Inventory Screen**: A grid showing the "power-ups" or "keys" (e.g., Goals, Risks, Tests) collected so far, with retro pixel borders.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Risk Framing**, **Project Recaps**, and **Lightening the Tone**. Use this after a heavy technical section to re-engage the audience.

## Style 08: Spotlight Quote Poster

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A theatrical stage where a single spotlight shines on a powerful quote. It is designed to create a dramatic pause, allowing the speaker's words to sink in.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Deep, dark charcoal or pure black (`#08080a`), with a soft, radial spotlight gradient centered on the text.
- **Ink**: Pure white for the quote, with a muted, elegant accent color (e.g., teal `#0d9488`) for the author's name.
- **Typography**: Large, elegant, italicized serif font (e.g., Playfair Display Italic) to convey literary authority and emotional resonance.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Centered Spotlight**: A 2-line quote centered in the middle of the screen, bathed in a soft radial gradient, with a tiny, spaced-out monospaced author label at the bottom.
- **The Left-Aligned Statement**: A large quote aligned to the left, taking up the left half of the screen, with the right half melting into deep shadow.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Closing Statements**, **Core Philosophies**, and **Speaker Pauses**. Use this when you want the audience to ponder a single profound sentence.
