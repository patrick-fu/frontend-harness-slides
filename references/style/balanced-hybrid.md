# Balanced Hybrid Styles (Styles 9 - 16)

Balanced Hybrid styles are designed to support both the live presenter and later readers. They contain a clear visual metaphor or structured layout that is easy to grasp live, but also hold enough supporting context to be readable and self-contained afterward.

"Good live slide, still readable afterward."

---

## Style 09: Signal Pipeline Flow

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A dark, precise technical map or network diagram showing data or signals moving through a routed pipeline. It emphasizes that the process is a structured system rather than magic.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Deep slate or space blue (`#020617` or `#0f172a`), with a subtle, dark grid pattern (`rgba(255,255,255,0.05)`).
- **Ink**: Glowing teal (`#0d9488`), emerald (`#10b981`), and warning yellow (`#eab308`) against a dark background.
- **Typography**: Clean, sharp monospaced fonts (e.g., Fira Code) for node labels and system status.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Horizontal Pipeline**: A sequence of dark, bordered panels representing system nodes, connected by glowing, animated directional arrows showing the data flow.
- **The Routed Hub**: A central "router" node with multiple incoming "raw input" lines and a single, clean "output" channel, decorated with status badges.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **System Architecture**, **Data Flow/Pipelines**, and **Technical Process Mapping**. Use this to explain how raw inputs are systematically processed into clean outputs.

## Style 10: Mechanical Scoring Funnel

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A colorful, mechanical pinball machine or multi-stage funnel. It represents the process of filtering, categorizing, and scoring messy inputs as they pass through structured lanes.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Deep dark blue (`#0f172a`) with vibrant, neon accents.
- **Ink**: Neon pink (`#ec4899`), yellow (`#eab308`), blue (`#3b82f6`), and green (`#10b981`).
- **Typography**: Bold sans-serif paired with a high-contrast monospaced font for score indicators.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Scoring Lanes**: A vertical stack of rounded panels representing "lanes", each with a colorful left border, a category title, and a score multiplier (e.g., "+500 PTS").
- **The Active Funnel**: A diagonal funnel layout where a "ball" (representing the active input) bounces through multiple pins and lands in a clean, structured slot.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Categorization**, **Multi-Stage Filtering**, and **Input Scoring/Prioritization**. Use this to make data filtering feel active and engaging.

## Style 11: Collaborative Pairing Board

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A clean, digital whiteboard where two distinct entities (e.g., human and AI, or frontend and backend) are pairing and collaborating. It focuses on division of labor, boundaries, and synchronization.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Clean, light gray (`#f8fafc`) or pure white (`#ffffff`).
- **Ink**: Deep slate (`#1e293b`) for text, paired with a calm, professional accent color (e.g., teal `#0f766e` or indigo `#4f46e5`).
- **Typography**: Clean, modern sans-serif (e.g., Inter) for structured lists, with subtle monospaced labels.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Split Board**: A two-column layout with a clean vertical divider. The left column shows the "Human Role/Intent" with a personal avatar, and the right column shows the "Agent Role/Execution" with a system icon.
- **The Sync Timeline**: A vertical timeline showing alternating steps between the two roles, with clear horizontal "sync points" where they align.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Pair-Programming Workflows**, **Role Division**, and **Process Synchronization**. Use this to explain how two different systems collaborate safely.

## Style 12: Studio Mixing Console

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A professional audio mixing console or studio dashboard. It turns the concept of parameter tuning, noise filtering, and signal balancing into physical faders, knobs, and level meters.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Deep, studio black (`#111827` or `#1f2937`).
- **Ink**: Metallic grays, with vibrant red (`#ef4444`), green (`#10b981`), and amber (`#f59e0b`) for faders and LED meters.
- **Typography**: Crisp, compact monospaced fonts (e.g., Fira Code) to mimic hardware labels.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Fader Rack**: A horizontal row of vertical fader tracks, where each track represents a specific parameter (e.g., Noise, Filler, Intent, Tests) with a colored knob positioned at its current level.
- **The Master Bus**: A split layout showing a bank of dial knobs on the left (for fine-tuning parameters) and a vertical LED level meter on the right showing the output quality.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Parameter Tuning**, **Multi-Factor Balancing**, and **Noise Filtering**. Use this to explain how multiple variables are balanced to achieve a clean result.

## Style 13: Transit Flow Subway Map

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A clean, systematic transit or subway map. It represents the idea that multiple independent streams of intent, constraints, and data are not isolated, but converge at key "transfer stations".

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Light, clean public-info gray (`#f1f5f9` or Tailwind `bg-slate-100`).
- **Ink**: Distinct, high-saturation transit line colors (e.g., Red Line `#ef4444`, Blue Line `#3b82f6`, Green Line `#10b981`), with dark slate for text.
- **Typography**: Highly legible, clean sans-serif (e.g., Inter) to mimic public transit signage.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Convergence Station**: Multiple colored lines entering from different sides of the screen and merging at a single, large, bordered circle representing the "Execution Station".
- **The Parallel Transit**: Two or three parallel horizontal lines representing different tracks (e.g., Code, Tests, Documentation), with vertical dotted lines showing synchronization checkpoints.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Multi-Track Workflows**, **Converging Processes**, and **Milestone Mapping**. Use this to show how different requirements merge into a single execution plan.

## Style 14: Kitchen Prep Station

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A warm, professional kitchen prep station. It treats raw, messy input data as "raw ingredients" and the preparation process as "chopping, trimming, and plating" to produce a clean, ready-to-serve output.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Soft, warm orange-tinted cream (`#fff7ed`).
- **Ink**: Rich terracotta orange (`#ea580c`), warm wood browns, and fresh herb green (`#10b981`).
- **Typography**: Friendly, rounded sans-serif or warm serif.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Prep Board**: A central, bordered panel representing a "cutting board", where raw text is shown on the left (with "filler" words crossed out in red), and the "trimmed, plated" clean output is shown on the right.
- **The Recipe Steps**: A vertical sequence of cards representing prep steps (e.g., Trim, Season, Plate), each with a food or utensil emoji and a short, actionable description.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Raw-to-Clean Transformations**, **Data Pre-processing**, and **Step-by-Step Refinement**. Use this to make data preparation feel tangible and easy to understand.

## Style 15: Context Bento Box

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A traditional, beautifully organized Japanese bento box. It represents the idea that a high-quality handoff or prompt must have distinct, compartmentalized sections, each holding a specific portion.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Deep, dark lacquer black or dark stone (`#1c1917` or `#111c24`).
- **Ink**: Natural stone grays, with warm amber (`#d97706`), cobalt blue (`#2563eb`), and emerald green (`#16a34a`) to color-code different compartments.
- **Typography**: Clean, balanced sans-serif paired with a light monospaced font for compartment labels.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The 4-Compartment Bento**: A non-symmetric grid of 4 rounded, dark-bordered panels (e.g., a large main compartment for the "Goal", and three smaller compartments for "Constraints", "Risks", and "Tests").
- **The Tiered Box**: A stacked vertical layout representing a multi-layered bento box, where sliding the top layer reveals deeper, more detailed technical specifications underneath.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Context Structuring**, **Handoff Specifications**, and **Multi-Category Overviews**. Use this to show how different pieces of information are neatly organized.

## Style 16: Diagnostic Kanban Board

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A developer's diagnostic board or Kanban board with reaction markers. It shows a system actively self-checking, tagging risks, and making uncertainty visible before taking action.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Deep, dark IDE slate (`#020617` or `#0f172a`).
- **Ink**: Dark slate panels, with vibrant status colors (e.g., success green `#10b981`, warning yellow `#f59e0b`, high-risk red `#ef4444`).
- **Typography**: Monospaced fonts (e.g., Fira Code) to create a developer-native, terminal-like atmosphere.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Self-Check List**: A vertical stack of diagnostic cards, where each card represents a check item (e.g., Goal Clarity, Payment Risk, Missing Repro) and displays a glowing, color-coded status badge (e.g., "CLEAR", "HIGH RISK", "UNCERTAIN").
- **The Risk Kanban**: Three columns representing "Safe", "Needs Verification", and "Blocked", with small, interactive cards that can be hovered over to reveal diagnostic details.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **System Diagnostics**, **Risk Assessment**, and **Developer-Native Workflows**. Use this to show how a system identifies and mitigates risks before execution.
