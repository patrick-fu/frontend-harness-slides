# Text Report Styles (Styles 17 - 24)

Text Report styles are designed for polished, evidence-rich, and self-contained reporting. They are ideal for async reviews, research readouts, technical documentation, and executive summaries where the slides must explain themselves without a live speaker.

"Self-contained enough for async review."

---

## Style 17: Research Memo

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A clean, modern academic paper, research brief, or executive memo. It prioritizes credibility, evidence, and clear data hierarchy, creating a calm, authoritative reading experience.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Pure, bright white (`#ffffff`) or light ivory, with crisp, thin rules (`#e2e8f0`). No grids or playful textures.
- **Ink**: Deep, professional slate or charcoal (`#0f172a`). Accent colors are extremely restrained (e.g., a single deep teal `#0f766e` or navy `#1e3a8a` for key metrics).
- **Typography**: Elegant serif font (e.g., Playfair Display) for main headers and quotes, paired with a highly readable sans-serif (e.g., Inter) for body text and data tables.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Executive Summary**: A classic memo layout with a formal header (Date, Author, Subject), followed by two columns: a left column for structured findings, and a right column for a large, highlighted callout metric.
- **The Evidence Block**: A large, italicized serif quote centered on the page, supported by a bottom row of three small, clean data cards.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Research Findings**, **Executive Summaries**, and **Bilingual/Academic Readouts**. Use this when your main goal is to establish credibility and present evidence.

## Style 18: Developer Ticket Brief

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A clean, structured developer issue, pull request, or ticket brief (similar to a polished GitHub or GitLab issue). It is ideal for engineering audiences because it makes tasks look actionable and reviewable.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Clean, light developer gray (`#f6f8fa` or `#f8fafc`) with thin, light borders (`#d0d7de`).
- **Ink**: Dark charcoal (`#24292f`), with a classic open-source green (`#2da44e`) or blue (`#0969da`) for status badges.
- **Typography**: Sharp sans-serif for headers, paired with a clean monospaced font for code snippets, file paths, and checklist items.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Actionable Issue**: A ticket-like layout with a green "Open" status badge and a bold title, followed by three distinct sections: "📋 Goal", "⚠️ Constraints", and "🧪 Verification Plan" (with monospaced checkboxes).
- **The PR Diff**: A split layout showing a raw, unorganized input on the left (red background), and a clean, structured, ticket-ready output on the right (green background).

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Engineering Tasks**, **Handoff Specifications**, and **Actionable Action Items**. Use this to present technical requirements in a format that developers and reviewers trust.

## Style 19: Decision Record

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A formal, lightweight Architectural Decision Record (ADR). It emphasizes engineering rigor, boundaries, trade-offs, and verification before taking action.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Structured, light blue-gray (`#f8fafc`) with thin, sharp rules (`#e2e8f0`).
- **Ink**: Deep slate (`#0f172a`), with a formal blue (`#2563eb`) or emerald (`#15803d`) for approval badges.
- **Typography**: Clean, structured monospaced fonts (e.g., Fira Code) to mimic official technical documentation.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The ADR Sheet**: A structured document layout with an "ADR-004: Approved" header, followed by four distinct, labeled blocks: "Context", "Decision", "Trade-offs", and "Verification".
- **The Trade-off Matrix**: A non-symmetric grid comparing two decisions, showing the immediate benefits and long-term maintenance costs of each.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Technical Decisions**, **Process Rigor**, and **Architectural Boundaries**. Use this to explain the "why" behind a complex engineering choice.

## Style 20: Benchmark Matrix

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A clean, analytical tool-evaluation matrix or comparison table. It shifts the evaluation away from superficial metrics and toward deep, structural capabilities.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Pure white (`#ffffff`) with alternating light gray rows (`#f8fafc`).
- **Ink**: Deep slate (`#1e293b`), with emerald green (`#0d9488`) for positive scores and red (`#ef4444`) for negative/missing features.
- **Typography**: Clean, highly legible sans-serif.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Comparison Table**: A classic matrix table with bold, dark headers, showing 3 to 4 evaluation criteria on the left, and comparing multiple tools or approaches in columns, using clear green checkmarks and red crosses.
- **The Metric Grid**: A 3-column grid of clean cards, where each card displays a single, massive benchmark score (e.g., "-40% Rework") and a short, explanatory paragraph.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Tool Comparisons**, **Evaluation Criteria**, and **Data-Heavy Benchmarks**. Use this when you need to present structured, comparative evidence.

## Style 21: Field Notes Report

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
An explorer's or researcher's field notebook. It feels tactile, practical, and observation-led, connecting digital workflows to physical environments and actual human habits.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Soft, aged paper yellow or light cream (`#fffbeb` or `#fbf8f0`).
- **Ink**: Dark sepia or charcoal, with warm amber (`#d97706`) or terracotta accents.
- **Typography**: A warm, legible handwriting or informal font (e.g., Caveat) for titles and margin notes, paired with a clean sans-serif for observations.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Field Log**: A notebook-like page with a "FIELD LOG: DESK SETUP" header, followed by a bulleted list of tactile, real-world observations (e.g., Room Noise, Desk Distance, Latency) with hand-drawn bullet points.
- **The Observation Sketch**: A split layout showing a simple, hand-drawn sketch of a physical setup on the left, and a structured list of behavioral observations on the right.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **User Research Observations**, **Physical Setup Notes**, and **Tactile Case Studies**. Use this to connect software concepts to real-world human behavior.

## Style 22: Operating Manual

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A compact, high-contrast industrial runbook, terminal guide, or operating manual. It turns complex processes into a repeatable, high-priority habit.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Deep, industrial black (`#111827` or `#090d16`).
- **Ink**: High-visibility warning yellow (`#eab308`) and crisp white.
- **Typography**: Bold, monospaced terminal fonts (e.g., Fira Code).

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Warning Runbook**: A terminal-like screen with a bold, yellow-and-black striped header ("⚠️ OPERATIONAL RUNBOOK: ACTIVE"), followed by a numbered sequence of 5 direct, actionable steps.
- **The Command Console**: A dark code block showing a terminal command execution, with a clean, annotated output explaining each parameter.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Actionable Runbooks**, **Repeatable Habits**, and **Command-Line Guides**. Use this to give the audience a clear, step-by-step "how-to" guide.

## Style 23: Checklist Ledger

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A plain, trustworthy financial or administrative ledger. It is intentionally restrained and practical, focusing entirely on completeness and readiness checks.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Very light, clean mint green (`#f4fbf7` or `#emerald-50/30`) with subtle, horizontal ledger lines.
- **Ink**: Deep forest green (`#064e3b`) for text, with bright green (`#15803d`) for checkmarks.
- **Typography**: Clean, traditional sans-serif or light serif.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Readiness Ledger**: A ledger-paper layout with a formal header ("📋 HANDOFF READINESS LEDGER"), followed by a list of strict checklist items, each starting with a green, bold checkmark `[✓]`.
- **The Audit Checklist**: A 2-column list of checkboxes, where checking an item reveals a small, green, inline explanation of why this check matters.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Readiness Checks**, **Acceptance Criteria**, and **Final Quality Checklists**. Use this to convey absolute trustworthiness and attention to detail.

## Style 24: Annotated Source & Diff

### 1. Aesthetic Metaphor
A high-fidelity code diff, source code reviewer, or text annotator. It is one of the clearest ways to explain product value because it shows the "before" and "after" transformation in a single view.

### 2. Color & Typographic Chemistry
- **Canvas**: Pure white (`#ffffff`) or light gray, with soft, tinted backgrounds for highlights (e.g., red `#fee2e2` for deletions, green `#dcfce7` for additions).
- **Ink**: Deep charcoal (`#0f172a`), with bright red (`#ef4444`) and emerald green (`#10b981`) for annotation markers.
- **Typography**: Sharp, clean monospaced fonts (e.g., Fira Code) for source text, paired with a clean sans-serif for annotations.

### 3. Suggested Layouts
- **The Before/After Diff**: A vertical stack of two panels. The top panel shows the "Raw Input" (with red highlights and strike-throughs), and the bottom panel shows the "Annotated Output" (with green highlights and bold, structured tags).
- **The Code Reviewer**: A central code block with hand-drawn circles or highlights around specific lines, with side-pointing arrows leading to explanatory sticky notes.

### 4. Semantic Fit Guide
Best suited for **Before/After Transformations**, **Code Diff Explanations**, and **Text Annotations**. Use this to visually prove the value of a transformation process.
